Laboratory of Computational Intelligence

We study how neural circuit dynamics support flexible, generalizable behavior.

Research

Our group combines theory, neural network modeling, and collaborations with experimental labs to understand how brains learn abstract structure and reuse it across tasks. We build biologically informed RNNs, analyze their dynamics, and generate testable predictions for neural recordings.

Current themes include:

  • Hierarchical decision making and context inference
  • Neural mechanisms of rule learning and generalization
  • Low-rank and cell-type-specific circuit models

  • Task generalization

    How can a circuit learn a small set of rules and apply them broadly?

  • Multi-timescale integration

    Mechanisms for slow context and fast choice.

  • Brain–AI parallels

    Using neuroscience to inspire efficient AI systems.
